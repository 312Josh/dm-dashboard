Guide: Understanding EOM Projections

How we use historical pacing to forecast monthly ARR

1. The Calculation (Example: DM on Working Day 12)

We determine the End of Month (EOM) Projection by dividing current results by our historical finish rate.

Current Actuals (Working Day 12): $135,000

STLY Pacing Historical Benchmark: 46.2% (The % of total ARR typically in by WD12)

EOM Projection: $292,208 ($135,000 ÷ 0.462)

2. Strategic Logic: 

Accounts for the "Hockey-Stick": ARR historically surges in the final week. This model builds in that "back-loading" surge rather than assuming a flat daily average.

Normalized for Timing: We track by Working Day instead of calendar date. This accounts for weekends and holidays, providing a cleaner YoY comparison.

Historical Mapping: Using last year’s performance curve (STLY) allows us to see exactly where we sit relative to "normal" seasonality.

3. Territory Accuracy 

We apply the Org Pacing (46.2% in this example) to each DM’s specific actuals to ensure a realistic forecast.

Individualized Data: If one DM is up 30% and another is down 30%, their specific projections reflect that reality.

Applying a flat "Org Lift" (e.g., +20%) to everyone would over-forecast underperformers and under-forecast rockstars.

Dashboard Note: While the bottom bar charts show the Org-wide % lift, the forecasting tool remains unique to each DM’s real-time performance.
